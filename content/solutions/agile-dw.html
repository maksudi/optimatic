Agile Data Warehousing

After two decades, the promise of the "data warehouse" has proven elusive. Organisations with large-scale investments in data warehouses (even if they've become dependent on the information) and organisations looking at new data warehouse initiatives or renewal programs look in dismay at both the cost and the timeframe. The good news is that packaged ERP solutions are offering more of the core Business Intelligence requirements on their platform.

The other good news is that cluster computing (either in the cloud or on-premise) offers an alternative data processing architecture, which means that data warehouses can spend less on expensive and uncertain software engineering. This allows you to apply simple tools and a lot of computer power to get answers when you think of the questions, not when the development backlog catches up.
