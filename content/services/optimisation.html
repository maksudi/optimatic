Optimisation

In the past few decades Australian companies have been under extreme pressures to deliver quality products and services at lower cost. This has been the case especially for the mining, manufacturing, and retail sectors. With the increase in competition, higher labour prices, and challenging economy, it's becoming more and more important to optimise processes, planning and scheduling, logistics, and transportation.

Take a minute to consider...

  • Could your organisation benefit from automatic workforce management?
  • Is your equipment being used in the most effective way?
  • Can you schedule different machines, taking maintenance into consideration, while keeping customers happy?
  • Is your supply chain locally optimised, i.e. do you make good decisions in parts of your supply chain but not the supply chain as a whole?
  • Do you need help with ingredient blending optimisation?

Our team has vast experience with these problems and can help you to achieve your goals.

How we approach the business of optimisation...

The team at Adaptic is passionate about optimisation. We employ the latest advances in artificial intelligence and operations research to help companies improve their operations. We have experience dealing with problems of varying complexity from route planning to complex supply chains where components interconnect and operate according to sophisticated rules and contingencies.

We start by building a model. While most optimisation solutions focus on linear models, the reality is that real world is non-linear. Therefore the linear models can suffer from precision errors and a failure to handle complexity. We have extensive experience in building high fidelity non-linear models and running state of the art optimisation algorithms on them. This way our clients will always have the most precise representation of their processes.

How optimisation works...

We believe that nature is the greatest supercomputer and we try to learn how nature solves problems. In our algorithms we mimic how ants, fish, and bees collaborate, how genetics works in the animal kingdom, the "survival of the fittest" principle, and how our brains solve complex problems. We're constantly "hungry" for the new state of the art research and use it to provide our clients with the best outcomes.

The decision support systems we implement not only work statically but overtime, they also learn about your problem, your preferences and your business, fine tuning themselves in an adaptive manner.

Why consider just one technique when we can run multiple algorithms simultaneously on the cluster and make them compete for the best solution for you, just like animals compete in nature for their survival.
